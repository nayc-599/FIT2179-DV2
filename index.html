1. Where Do Australia's Emigrants Come From?

Every year, hundreds of thousands of people born overseas leave Australia and return to their home countries. The flow map below shows the largest emigration corridors — China, India and the United Kingdom dominate, reflecting Australia's multicultural ties across Asia and Europe. The darker a country is shaded, the more emigrants it has sent.

Asia accounts for the majority of emigrant departures — with China alone contributing over 590,000 people across the 21 years of this dataset.

Breaking it down by country, ten nations account for the lion's share of departures. China has surged ahead of the pack over the past two decades, driven by a wave of students and temporary residents returning home. The United Kingdom and New Zealand follow — longstanding ties that reflect Australia's historical migration patterns.


China alone contributes over 590,000 departures — nearly double the United Kingdom in second place.

2. How Have Rankings Changed Over Time?

These rankings were not always fixed. China's rise was dramatic — surging from second in 2014 to an undisputed first by 2019, driven by a wave of students and temporary residents returning home. Then COVID-19 hit and reshuffled the order entirely. Press Play to watch the decade unfold.

The COVID year (2020–21) pulled every ribbon toward the bottom at once — but recovery was uneven. India and Philippines bounced back strongest; Malaysia and USA still lag behind pre-pandemic levels.

3. Where in Australia Are They Leaving From?

Most emigrant departures flow from the states where most people live. New South Wales, Victoria and Queensland dominate — but every state and territory contributes to the national total. All three largest states show the same sharp COVID dip in 2020–21, followed by a gradual recovery that is still ongoing.

New South Wales accounts for the largest share of national departures — consistent with being Australia's most populous and internationally connected state.

4. The Economic Angle — Remittances Over Time

Migration is never just about people — money follows. The same countries sending emigrants from Australia are also among the world's largest recipients of personal remittances. India's remittance income dwarfs every other country, while China and the Philippines also rank among the global top recipients. Hover over the chart to highlight individual countries.

Even through COVID-19 — when emigrant departures collapsed — remittances kept growing for most countries. The economic connection between Australia and its diaspora source nations proved more resilient than migration itself.

5. Departures vs Remittances — A Continental Comparison

Comparing departures and remittances side by side across continents reveals a striking divergence. Asia dominates on both measures — but the two charts tell different stories. On departures, the Asian countries are relatively clustered. On remittances, India towers above every other country in this dataset — a reflection of its enormous global diaspora.

India receives over $119 billion in remittances globally yet ranks only fourth in Australian departures — the largest gap between migration volume and economic footprint of any country in this dataset.
